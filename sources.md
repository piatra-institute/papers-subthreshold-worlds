## Bibliography

All entries verified in a dedicated pass against journals / arXiv / SEP / DOI (2026-07-01); corrections noted (SEP entry is Raffman & Hyde, latest edition; Dzhafarov & Perry, not Dzhafarov & Dzhafarov; Bonnasse-Gahot & Nadal published in Neural Computation 2022). The SEP sorites entry itself is used only as background and cited in prose without a formal bibliography line to keep the reference list to primary/authoritative sources.

Abramsky, S., and Brandenburger, A. (2011). The sheaf-theoretic structure of non-locality and contextuality. *New Journal of Physics*, 13, 113036. — the rigorous sheaf-theory of contextuality; local sections that fail to glue globally (the §8 local-to-global frame).

Bonnasse-Gahot, L., and Nadal, J.-P. (2022). Categorical perception: a groundwork for deep learning. *Neural Computation*, 34(2), 437–475. — information-theoretic categorical perception: within-category compression, between-category separation (the readout's coarsening).

Brunet, T., and King, N. (2017). The origin of animal multicellularity and cell differentiation. *Developmental Cell*, 43(2), 124–140. — the local(cell)-to-global(individual) transition; §8.

Burkitt, A. N. (2006). A review of the integrate-and-fire neuron model: I. Homogeneous synaptic input. *Biological Cybernetics*, 95(1), 1–19. — the integrate-and-fire abstraction; latent membrane accumulation to a spike readout.

Cobreros, P., Égré, P., Ripley, D., and van Rooij, R. (2012). Tolerant, classical, strict. *Journal of Philosophical Logic*, 41(2), 347–385. — strict-tolerant logic: keep the tolerant step, deny the chained classical proof (the §3 diagnosis).

Dzhafarov, E. N., and Perry, L. (2014). Perceptual matching and sorites: experimental study of an ancient Greek paradox. *Attention, Perception, and Psychophysics*, 76(8), 2441–2464. — perceptual matching is probabilistic; locally-matched stimuli with discriminable endpoints (sorites in the lab).

Feigenson, L., Dehaene, S., and Spelke, E. (2004). Core systems of number. *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*, 8(7), 307–314. — two core numerical systems (small-set individuation; approximate magnitude).

Fine, K. (1975). Vagueness, truth and logic. *Synthese*, 30(3–4), 265–300. — supervaluationism; one of the older families §3 situates.

Harnad, S. (Ed.). (1987). *Categorical Perception: The Groundwork of Cognition*. Cambridge University Press. — categorical perception as the perceptual coarsening.

Hodgkin, A. L., and Huxley, A. F. (1952). A quantitative description of membrane current and its application to conduction and excitation in nerve. *Journal of Physiology*, 117(4), 500–544. — the neuron as a continuous latent integrator with a spike readout.

Hyde, D. C. (2011). Two systems of non-symbolic numerical cognition. *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience*, 5, 150. — subitization / parallel individuation vs the approximate number system.

Jaeger, H., and Haas, H. (2004). Harnessing nonlinearity: predicting chaotic systems and saving energy in wireless communication. *Science*, 304(5667), 78–80. — echo-state / reservoir computing; history in a high-dimensional latent state (§6).

Knoll, A. H. (2011). The multiple origins of complex multicellularity. *Annual Review of Earth and Planetary Sciences*, 39, 217–239. — complex multicellularity arose independently several times (a reachable transition).

Lukoševic̆ius, M., and Jaeger, H. (2009). Reservoir computing approaches to recurrent neural network training. *Computer Science Review*, 3(3), 127–149. — the unifying review of reservoir computing as a model class.

Maass, W., Natschläger, T., and Markram, H. (2002). Real-time computing without stable states: a new framework for neural computation based on perturbations. *Neural Computation*, 14(11), 2531–2560. — liquid state machines; the spiking reservoir tying the neuron to §6.

Mac Lane, S., and Moerdijk, I. (1992). *Sheaves in Geometry and Logic: A First Introduction to Topos Theory*. Springer. — the sheaf/topos machinery behind the local-to-global framing.

Nieder, A., and Miller, E. K. (2004). A parieto-frontal network for visual numerical information in the monkey. *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, 101(19), 7457–7462. — a neural population code for numerosity; number as estimate beyond subitizing.

Raffman, D. (2014). *Unruly Words: A Study of Vague Language*. Oxford University Press. — the psychophysically-informed, multiple-range theory of vagueness.

Scheffer, M., Bascompte, J., Brock, W. A., Brovkin, V., Carpenter, S. R., Dakos, V., Held, H., van Nes, E. H., Rietkerk, M., and Sugihara, G. (2009). Early-warning signals for critical transitions. *Nature*, 461(7260), 53–59. — critical slowing down: rising variance/autocorrelation before a tipping point (§7).

Szathmáry, E., and Maynard Smith, J. (1995). The major evolutionary transitions. *Nature*, 374(6519), 227–232. — the major-transitions framework; new units of individuality (§8).

Williamson, T. (1994). *Vagueness*. Routledge. — epistemicism; the sharp-but-unknowable line §3 situates.
